Exercise and Mental Health

Physical activity does not only improve physical health. Exercise can also reduce stress, improve mood, and support long-term emotional well-being.

This Mayo Clinic video explains how exercise can support brain health and reduce the risk of several health conditions.

Watch Mayo Clinic Minute: Exercise and Your Brain

The Mental Effects of Inactivity

Long periods of inactivity can increase stress, anxiety, emotional fatigue, and depression. Students and workers who spend hours sitting or looking at screens may experience mental exhaustion and low motivation over time.

How Exercise Helps the Brain

“People who exercise experience about 43% fewer poor mental health days.”

— Chekroud et al.

Exercise releases chemicals called endorphins, which help improve mood and lower stress levels. Physical activity also improves sleep quality and gives people a mental break from daily pressure and responsibilities.

Mental Health Benefits of Exercise

  • Improves mood
  • Helps manage stress
  • Supports better sleep
  • Reduces anxiety
  • Increases focus and motivation
  • Provides a healthy emotional outlet

Everyday Examples

Many people use exercise as a way to manage stress in daily life. Going for a walk, exercising at the gym after work, or completing a short workout at home can help clear the mind and improve emotional well-being.
