The Problem: Physical Inactivity

Physical inactivity has become a growing health concern as more people spend long periods sitting for school, work, and daily life.

What Causes Inactivity?

Busy schedules, screen time, online classes, office jobs, and long periods of sitting can make it harder for people to include movement in their daily routines.

How It Affects the Body

When the body does not get regular movement, muscles can become weaker, blood flow can decrease, and the heart may have to work harder. Over time, these changes can increase the risk of chronic health problems.

Health Risks Linked to Inactivity

“Physical inactivity is linked to over 40 chronic diseases.”

— Ruegsegger and Booth

  • Heart disease
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Higher blood sugar levels
  • Muscle weakness
  • Poor circulation
  • Lower quality of life

Why This Problem Matters

The effects of inactivity can build over time. Without consistent physical activity, people may experience reduced endurance, lower mobility, and a greater chance of developing serious health complications.
